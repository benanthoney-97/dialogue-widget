Figure 2 — PIQ → Frequency → Intention → AI Use

This figure presents the estimated path coefficients for the model linking Perceived Information Quality (PIQ) to Behavioural Intention and Artificial Intelligence Use, with Frequency acting as a key mediator.

Model summary

  • PIQ constructs (Credibility, Reliability, Importance) predict Frequency of GenAI interactions.
  • Frequency predicts Behavioural Intention to use GenAI.
  • Behavioural Intention predicts actual AI Use.
  • Some direct paths from PIQ → Intention are shown; verify sign/significance with the text.

Implications

  • Importance appears to be the strongest PIQ driver of Frequency, followed by Credibility; Reliability is weaker.
  • The Frequency → Intention link (.36) supports the idea that hands-on engagement is a key route to adoption.
  • Behavioural Intention meaningfully predicts actual use (.35), consistent with adoption theories.

How to narrate this figure

“Students who judge GenAI information as important and credible tend to use it more frequently. That frequency raises their intention to keep using it, which then translates into actual usage. Direct paths from PIQ to intention are present but appear smaller; full stats should be confirmed in the results table.”
