Figure 1 — Proposed Research Model (Explanation)

The diagram groups three information–quality constructs inside Perceived Information Quality (PIQ) and connects them through Frequency to Behavioural Intention and ultimately to Artificial Intelligence Use.

At a glance

  • Credibility, Reliability and Importance (PIQ) each have a direct positive effect on Frequency of student interactions with GenAI.
  • Frequency has a direct positive effect on Behavioural Intention.
  • Behavioural Intention has a direct positive effect on Artificial Intelligence Use (actual usage).
  • Thus, Frequency mediates the influence of PIQ on intention, and Behavioural Intention mediates the path to actual use.

Constructs

ConstructRole in modelPlain-language meaning
Credibility PIQ Exogenous predictor → Frequency Students perceive GenAI outputs as trustworthy and from legitimate sources.
Reliability PIQ Exogenous predictor → Frequency GenAI responses are consistent and accurate for the task at hand.
Importance PIQ Exogenous predictor → Frequency Information provided by GenAI is valuable/relevant for HE study goals.
Frequency Mediator → Behavioural Intention How often students interact with GenAI tools in their coursework.
Behavioural Intention Mediator → AI Use Stated likelihood/plans to use GenAI in the near term.
Artificial Intelligence Use Outcome variable Observed self-reported or logged use of GenAI in HE tasks.

Implied hypotheses

  • H1–H3: Credibility, Reliability, and Importance each positively predict Frequency.
  • H4: Frequency positively predicts Behavioural Intention.
  • H5: Behavioural Intention positively predicts AI Use.
  • Mediation: (Credibility/Reliability/Importance) → Frequency → Intention → AI Use.

Why this differs from classic TAM

The model reframes TAM by replacing Perceived Ease of Use and Perceived Usefulness with PIQ dimensions more specific to GenAI content quality (credibility, reliability, importance), making usage frequency the proximal driver of intention.

How to read the original diagram

  1. Start at the left: the three PIQ boxes sit inside a dashed container labelled “Perceived Information Quality (PIQ)”.
  2. Each PIQ box points to Frequency (three arrows converging).
  3. Frequency points to Behavioural Intention, which points to Artificial Intelligence Use.
  4. Arrowheads imply direction and positive influence; no feedback loops are drawn.

Measurement suggestions (examples)

  • Credibility/Reliability/Importance: 3–5 Likert items per construct (validated PIQ scales).
  • Frequency: self-reported weekly use or platform logs.
  • Intention: standard BI items (e.g., “I intend to use GenAI next term…”).
  • AI Use: observed usage counts or task-level adoption metrics.
